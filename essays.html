The flaw in ambitions

Often ambitions are understood as an abnormally strong desire for something. As a powerful urge to reach one's desires and dreams. Their meaning is no farther than that. To get an insight about them, one could take a look at his own wishes. A tip to it, they can't be limited by time, place, status, class etc.… any person with a life to pedal has one. Starting from an ancient farmer to a very busy man living on the 55th floor of a building in Manhattan. The ambition being pushy might be a sufficient driving force for success. But still, they have flaws in them. One is that they can turn into obsessions, and worse- into unreachable fantasy land. That case happens when a person fails to see that he should strive to achieve his ambitions.

The conflicts in the teenage mind

“A person is like a dense forest thicket, overgrown with a twisting mess of vines, weeds, shrubs, saplings and flowers. No person has only one desire. They have many and usually, those desires conflicted with one another like two rose bushes fighting for the same patch of ground” Emperor's soul

“Such wise words!” was my first impression when I read this quote. Indeed, people, in general, have this inevitable habit of second-guessing themselves. so many different ideas try to make our minds their destination. Especially teenagers like ourselves, find it hard to reconcile our conflicting thoughts and form a coherent perspective towards life.

Have you ever had any trouble in expressing yourself because you could label your incoming thoughts in clearly distinguished packets? Well, if you do, you have chosen just the right booklet to help you understand! See, our mind, simply put, is an amazing creation. We observe, we listen and we interact with our immediate environment. And all the information we gather is being accumulated, forming a hoard of knowledge in our psyche. It is what we do ever since we were introduced to this mysterious world. Our mind, being the coordinator and the responsible one, draws conclusions and present us a whole bunch of striking ideas. Not to confuse us, mind you, it is just to help. (Don't judge 😊) but n the process, we feel overwhelmed. That is why we need to build a structured and strong fortress around our head. How do we do that? We need to be flexible enough to entertain many ideas but still be able to keep our head straight.

So read books! Read as many books as you can! Whenever you encounterer a fictional character facing difficulties, warring with themselves and finally coming out of it victorious, you get an insight as to how you lead your life. Always remember that character has to be established through a thousand stumbles. But reading books make those stumbles less painful since you are expecting them anyway. Therefore, whenever you are stuck with conflicting ideas, try to look at them in a detached manner, as if you are witnessing someone's else's revelation. (this is t help us not to be biased by our personal emotions and whatnots.) and then draw parallels from your past experience, from books, from your parents'/ guardian's experience that you know of, ad try, to the best of your ability, to organize and reflect on these thoughts. if not, consult others, preferably your teachers and your close kins, to help you decide. There is nothing wrong with asking for help. I am reasonably sure this will alleviate our confusion.
