Clubs

The Book Club

The book club, whose aim is to promote reading habits to the society, has been active since its creation last year. In the summer, its members volunteered to help students in Sile Enat charity organization. “It was fun helping the children read, although it was challenging as they had difficulties in understanding English” cheers Liya Daniel, a volunteer in the club. The club's activities are not only limited to volunteering though, but it also collaborates with the social concerns club and toastmasters club. Together, they help students practice public speaking. Every two weeks, members are encouraged to make presentations on any topic.

Career club

Career club was founded this year in order for five students to have the chance to develop their future careers. To do so, the club invites guests from different fields and gains information on their profession and their life experience. When the club held its first club meeting, the coordinators invited Mahlet. She is a medical doctor and her presentation was really helpful especially for those students who pursue to study medicine in college.

After that, a politician named Lelisa was invited. Similarly, her presentation was impressive and she was a good example for girls who want to study politics which is considered to be hard for them to study. Overall, the club is actively going and there are many more activities to come.

Future Health Professionals Club

“There were no clubs that help people choose their major throughout high school, therefore it made us find this club, “ says Icy, one of the founders of the FHP club. It aims to help cathedral students consider the pros and cons of studying medicine and health, and make an informed decision if they want to study them. “there are some students who decide to study medicine just because they think it might get them a job. We believe that students should have some experience and knowledge of studying medicine before they decide to study it,”.

Club members were invited to attend Dr.----- presentation of her life as a medicine student along with different, good and bad, aspects of it. She said that studying medicine is not extremely hard, but it not for the ones with no strong determination. Presentations like this will help achieve the club's goals.

In the near future, the club is considering to include other activities too. it is considering to make partnerships with the red cross-organization, doing volunteer work, and contribute to improving student's health in some ways including sanitation.

Awareness Club

Q: What is the awareness club
A: Awareness club is a club we created so that the society can be awoken one. Since there are a lot of people unaware, even though they don't mean to be, we are trying to teach them to give attention to the things that are important.
Although the club is new (created this year by a group of 11th-grade students ), it is one of the clubs that has been actively participating in this past few weeks. And by having the opportunity, I interviewed the founders of the new awareness club.

Some time at the beginning of this year, they had a fundraising activity. Getting the money without anything to offer, seemed so dull and after all, wasn't the aim for their club. So they draw on the idea of the cards. 'As our noble, we wanted to awake the society', and so they did. Making students write the names and section of the students they want to give to and secretly (by making the writer anonymous), they delivered the cards with chocolates and lollipops.

Students teasing each other, teachers, interested and eager to know what was going on, made their effort collectively speaking their club, fruitful. Now that they have achieved that fundraising, they are planning on having another one on Christmas. 'For Christmas, we are planning on helping people to choose what gifts they can give to their friends and we will also deliver gifts by taking orders on our telegram channel, @awarenessclub and we hope for the best. And by having this opportunity, we would like to thank our supervisor for everything, teacher Gold (English teacher)'.

Plus Factor Club

Very engaging communicators, Baron and Crisp, the two founders of The Plus Factor Club were interviewed by the Cathe Times.

The plus Factor club founded this year has been working well for the past few months, when asked about the meaning behind the name, Baron replied with a simple “Plus is the sign of positivity, and that's what we're trying to do here, imbed positivity in the minds of the teenagers”, she continues to describe why they decided to set up this club, 'We want to help the students prepare for what they want to become in the future, we want them to get a glimpse of what they'll go through in that certain field, they want to pursue and We hope they learn from the mistakes the people we invited have gone through and handle them differently”.She continued, "It was not so difficult to set up this club, the students were excited and motivated, there were no negative replies from our peers. The only problem we faced was with the school administration who were not co-operating very well. But we finally managed to get Teacher Zed to be the supervisor.""

"When registering the members, it was a bit confusing to distinguish between the people who really wanted to be a part of it and the people who wanted it just for recommendation and certificate. But we managed by observing the students who participated well and paid their club fees, attended the club meetings and had a contribution to the club. We've had the chance to invite a professional psychologist who was educated in Germany now working in US AID. We plan on making this into a movement".
